All three proteins show low pLDDT (overall mean 0.8). This is the expected and scientifically correct result for these specific proteins — it is not a modelling failure.

  • Alpha7 nAChR (mean 0.8) — This is a pentameric transmembrane receptor. ESMFold predicts a single chain in water; without the membrane environment and four partner subunits, the model cannot be confident. The low score reflects the missing biological context, not a poorly folded protein.
  • RIC3_CAEEL (mean 0.6) and RIC3_HUMAN (mean 0.9) — RIC-3 proteins are intrinsically disordered (IDPs). Low pLDDT for IDPs is the correct prediction: these proteins genuinely lack a stable fold in isolation and only adopt structure upon binding a client protein such as alpha7.
  • Key finding: RIC3_CAEEL and RIC3_HUMAN have similar pLDDT scores ({ric3_ce} vs {ric3_hu}). Equally disordered chaperones suggest conserved functional mechanism — a positive signal that C. elegans RIC-3 may retain the interaction motifs needed to chaperone human alpha7.
  • Any blue/teal regions visible in the structures are the genuinely structured domains — transmembrane helices in alpha7, coiled-coil regions in RIC-3. These are the functionally critical segments.
Overall conclusion: Low pLDDT does not mean C. elegans RIC-3 cannot fold human alpha7 — it means single-chain ESMFold cannot answer that question. The answer requires modelling the complex.
